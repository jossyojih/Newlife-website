The Power of Sustainable Research, Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence

High level research where scientists formulate hypotheses and drive home results has placed humans above other creatures. The standard of living continues to increase from century to century. With the revolution in science and technology, therapies have been developed for incurable diseases.

Other areas such as housing, transportation, crop breeding, renewable energy, telecommunication, electronics, etc, have received much improvement in the 21st century.  In the past decade, artificial intelligence (AI) has revolutionized medicine & healthcare, agriculture, communication, marketing, speech recognition, self-driving cars and many other sectors. With DeepMind’s protein structure prediction known as AlphaFold (AlphaFold), AI has proven to solve one of the most difficult human tasks.

Machine learning (ML) and Natural language processing (NLP) are some of the branches of artificial intelligence. Machine learning is divided into supervised learning (e.g Random Forest, XGBoost, Support Vector Machines, etc) and supervised learning (e.g Deep learning, clustering, principal component analysis, etc). While advances in NLP have been able to make meaning out of large text datasets generated, with the advent of Transformers and Transfer learning, much more will be achieved in this decade.

With ChatGPT developed by OpenAI, Generative adversarial networks (GANs) hold promise in different sectors including medicine and healthcare. Explainable and interpretable AI/ML methods such as LIME, SHAP, Anchors, and new approaches are needed to explain the complexity of algorithms and interpret the ‘Black Box’ model.
